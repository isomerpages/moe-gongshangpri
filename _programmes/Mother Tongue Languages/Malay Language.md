<h4><strong>Malay Language Curriculum</strong></h4>
<p>The Malay Language Curriculum (Primary) was developed by MOE to achieve
our education philosophy which is to nurture knowledgeable person, active
contributor and morally upright individuals. This philosophy is in line
with the vision of the teaching and learning of Malay Language that is
the Vision of Arif Budiman, namely the learned person who contributes to
society. The vision sets the direction for teachers to develop the language
skills and knowledge, and to internalise the values and culture.</p>
<p>The ML syllabus focus on listening, speaking, reading, writing, written
interaction as well as oral interaction skills.&nbsp; These skills are
taught so that pupils are able to communicate and interact effectively
and with confidence.</p>

</p>
<p>The Malay Language department uses student-centered approach, which focuses
on teaching Malay Language in a fun and interactive manner.&nbsp;&nbsp;This
will help pupils to be active and competent users of the language.&nbsp;Differentiated
learning is adopted to cater to pupils with different learning profile
and ability. This is to gradually build up pupils’ competency and understanding
in the language. Explicit teaching of Malay Language will help pupils to
improve on their mastery of the language.</p>

<h3><strong>Programmes </strong></h3>
<p><strong>P1 MTL Read with Me Programme (ML session)</strong>
</p>
<p>This programme provides a platform for parents to bond with their child
and cultivate a love for reading by making reading an enjoyable and meaningful
activity. During this programme, students learn their Mother Tongue Language
through a fun, engaging and interactive story telling session followed
by a craft-making activity for both parent and child to work together on.
Parents too learn tips and ideas on how to strengthen parent-child bonding
through shared reading experiences at home.</p>

<p><strong>P4&amp;5 HML Petah Pintar - Hosting &amp; Presentation Skills (ALP)</strong>
</p>
<p>Petah Pintar aims to develop effective oral communication to inspire students
to gain self-confidence and be well-equipped with the skills to interact
with an audience. Using role-playing as a means of self-expression, Petah
Pintar helps students to develop good public speaking skills. Students
will be immersed in the art of effective communication, learn presenting
skills and explore their own style to host with confidence.</p>

<p><strong>National Malay Writing Competition</strong>
</p>
<p>Our students took part in the Primary 5 and 6 category of the National
Malay Writing Competition. This annual competition celebrates and nurtures
local literary talent. It unites participants from various backgrounds
and age groups, offering them a platform to showcase their creativity,
mastery of the Malay language and deep passion for writing.</p>

<br><strong>P6 Cultural Camp</strong>
</p>
<p>The Malay pupils participated in two experiential activities which are
the Wayang Kulit with Puppet Making and Die-cut Rumah Kampung Model Making,
to strengthen language proficiency and cultural appreciation. Through the
Wayang Kulit activity, pupils applied oral communication skills such as
voice projection and intonation in Malay language while collaborating to
perform a simple presentation. The Rumah Kampung model-making activity
enabled pupils to learn about traditional house structures and cultural
vocabulary through hands-on learning, promoting critical thinking collaboration
and self-management.</p>
